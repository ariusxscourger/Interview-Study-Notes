## TOPIC: Salary Negotiation & Offer Evaluation Mastery

### WHY? (Problem Statement)

**Most engineers leave 10-30% on the table:**
- Never negotiate (fear, discomfort, impostor syndrome)
- Accept first offer without counter
- Focus only on base salary, ignore total compensation
- Don't understand equity, refreshers, tax implications
- No leverage preparation

**What Negotiation Mastery Solves:**
| Problem | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| Fear of losing offer | Data-driven confidence |
| Don't know market rate | Research + benchmarks |
| Uncomfortable asking | Scripts + practice |
| Single offer = no leverage | Create leverage (timeline, alternatives) |
| Sign without understanding | Total comp analysis framework |

#### **EQUITY VALUATION GUIDE**

```
PUBLIC COMPANY RSU:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Value = Shares × Current_Stock_Price
Vesting: Typically 4 years, 25%/yr, monthly/quarterly after 1yr cliff
Refresh: Annual grant (often 25-50% of initial)
Risk: Stock volatility, liquidation preference

PRIVATE COMPANY EQUITY (ISO/NSO):
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Value = Shares × (Current_409A_Price - Strike_Price)
⚠️  HIGH RISK: Illiquid, 90% expire worthless
Key Questions:
  □ % Ownership = Shares / Fully_Diluted_Outstanding
  □ Last 409A valuation date & price
  □ Strike price vs current 409A
  □ Liquidation preference (1x, 2x, participating?)
  □ Anti-dilution provisions
  □ Exercise window post-termination (90 days standard)
  □ Early exercise option (83(b) election)
  □ Tender offer history / secondary market

EQUITY COMPARISON TABLE:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
| Company | Grant | Price | Vest | Annualized | % Ownership | Risk |
|---------|-------|-------|------|------------|-------------|------|
| Public  | 1000  | $150  | 4yr  | $37.5K     | 0.001%      | Low  |
| Unicorn | 5000  | $20   | 4yr  | $25K       | 0.01%       | Med  |
| Series A| 50000 | $1.50 | 4yr  | $18.75K    | 0.1%        | High |
| Series B| 20000 | $5    | 4yr  | $25K       | 0.02%       | Med  |
```

### PATTERNS & ANTI-PATTERNS

| Pattern | Anti-Pattern | Why Avoid |
|---------|--------------|-----------|
| **Research first, then talk** | Wing it | Data = leverage |
| **Holistic negotiation** | Base only | Equity often > base long-term |
| **Create leverage** | Single offer | Parallel processes = power |
| **Call recruiter** | Email negotiation | Tone lost, no real-time adjustment |
| **Get it in writing** | Verbal promises | Unenforceable |
| **Counter with data** | "I want more" | "Market data shows X for this role" |
| **Negotiate holistically** | Base only | Equity, sign-on, refresh, benefits |
| **Know walk-away** | No floor | Desperation shows |
| **Build recruiter relationship** | Adversarial | They advocate for you internally |
| **Ask for time** | Immediate decision | Pressure tactic |

### INTERVIEW QUESTIONS (Negotiation Scenarios)

#### **1. "What are your salary expectations?"**
```
BAD: "I want $200K"
GOOD: "Based on my research for Senior Engineer roles at Series B companies in 
      this market, the range is $180-220K base. Given my experience with [specific 
      tech/impact], I'm targeting the upper half. I'm flexible on the mix of base 
      vs equity. What's the band for this role?"
```

#### **2. "We don't have budget for that base."**
```
RESPONSE: "I understand budget constraints. Let's look at the total package. 
           If base is fixed at $X, can we bridge the gap with:
           - Higher equity grant (current grant is X shares, market is Y)
           - Sign-on bonus (common for Senior+ roles)
           - Guaranteed first-year refresh grant
           - Accelerated review cycle (6mo vs 12mo)
           What's feasible within your constraints?"
```

#### **3. "We can't go higher on equity."**
```
RESPONSE: "I understand equity pools have limits. Can we explore:
           - Higher sign-on bonus (common for Senior/Staff)
           - Annual refresh grant commitment in writing
           - Performance-based equity accelerators
           - Learning/development budget ($5-10K/yr)
           - Extra PTO or flexible work arrangement
           What combination works within your framework?"
```

#### **4. "We need an answer by tomorrow."**
```
RESPONSE: "I'm excited about this opportunity and want to give it the 
           consideration it deserves. This is a significant career decision. 
           Can I have until [specific day, 3-5 business days] to review the 
           written offer, discuss with my family/mentor, and get back to you 
           with any questions? I'll be responsive throughout."
```

#### **5. "What's your current salary?" (Illegal in many states)**
```
RESPONSE: "I'm not comfortable sharing my current compensation as I believe 
           compensation should be based on the value I bring to this role and 
           market rates. I'm happy to share my expectations: I'm targeting 
           $X-$Y total comp for a Senior Engineer role based on market data."
```

### GOTCHAS & EDGE CASES

- [ ] **Clawback clauses** - Sign-on/relocation often clawback <12-24 months
- [ ] **Equity cliff** - 1 year cliff standard, negotiate 6-month or immediate vest
- [ ] **Refresh grants** - Not guaranteed, get commitment in writing
- [ ] **409A vs Preferred** - Common vs Preferred price gap at private companies
- [ ] **Liquidation preference** - 1x non-participating standard, watch for 2x+
- [ ] **AMT on ISO** - Exercise early (83b) or plan for AMT hit
- [ ] **Clawback on promo** - Some companies claw back unvested on promo
- [ ] **Remote pay bands** - Geographic differentials (15-30% adjustments)
- [ ] **Non-compete/Non-solicit** - Enforceability varies by state (CA = void)
- [ ] **IP assignment** - Side projects ownership, pre-existing inventions

#### 1. **Total Comp Spreadsheet Formula**

```python
# Python model for offer comparison
class Offer:
    def __init__(self, name, base, bonus_pct, equity_shares, 
                 stock_price, vest_years, sign_on=0, 
                 refresh_shares=0, refresh_price=None):
        self.name = name
        self.base = base
        self.annual_bonus = base * bonus_pct
        self.equity_annual = (equity_shares * stock_price) / vest_years
        self.refresh_annual = (refresh_shares * (refresh_price or stock_price)) / 4
        self.sign_on = sign_on
    
    def year1_total(self):
        return self.base + self.annual_bonus + self.equity_annual + \
               self.refresh_annual + self.sign_on
    
    def steady_state(self):
        return self.base + self.annual_bonus + self.equity_annual + \
               self.refresh_annual

# Usage
offers = [
    Offer("Company A", 180000, 0.15, 5000, 150, 4, 25000, 1000, 150),
    Offer("Company B", 160000, 0.20, 20000, 25, 4, 50000, 5000, 20),
]

for o in offers:
    print(f"{o.name}: Year 1 = ${o.year1_total():,.0f}, "
          f"Steady = ${o.steady_state():,.0f}")
```

#### 2. **Equity Value Calculator**

```python
def equity_value(shares, price, vest_years, cliff_months=12, 
                 monthly_vest=True):
    """
    Calculate monthly/annual equity value with cliff
    """
    if monthly_vest:
        monthly_shares = shares / (vest_years * 12)
        cliff_shares = monthly_shares * cliff_months
    else:
        monthly_shares = shares / (vest_years * 4)
        cliff_shares = monthly_shares * (cliff_months // 3)
    
    annual_value = (shares / vest_years) * price
    monthly_value = annual_value / 12
    
    return {
        'total_value': shares * price,
        'annual_value': annual_value,
        'monthly_value': monthly_value,
        'cliff_value': cliff_shares * price,
        'post_cliff_monthly': monthly_shares * price
    }

# Example: 5000 RSU @ $150, 4yr vest, 1yr cliff
print(equity_value(5000, 150, 4))
# {'annual_value': 187500, 'monthly_value': 15625, 'cliff_value': 46875, ...}
```

### ANKI FLASHCARDS

| Front | Back |
|-------|------|
| TC Formula | Base + Bonus + Equity (annualized) + Sign-on + Refresh + Benefits |
| RSU Annualized | (Shares × Price) / Vest_Years |
| Private Equity Value | Shares × (409A_Price - Strike_Price) |
| 409A vs Preferred | Common (employee) vs Preferred (investor) - 10-50% discount typical |
| Liquidation Pref | 1x non-participating standard. 2x+ or participating = bad |
| 83(b) Election | File within 30 days of exercise. Pay tax on spread NOW vs later |
| AMT on ISO | Exercise early in year, sell same year to avoid AMT trap |
| Refresh Grant | Annual equity top-up. Get commitment in writing! |
| Sign-On Clawback | Usually 12-24 months. Prorated repayment if leave early. |
| Geographic Differential | 15-30% adjustment for remote. SF/NYC = 100%, Denver = 85% |
| Negotiation Leverage | Competing offers > Market data > Unique skills > Nothing |
| Recruiter Relationship | They advocate internally. Build rapport, not adversarial. |
| Counter with Data | "Levels.fyi shows P50 for Staff at Series B is $X" |
| Holistic Negotiation | Base + Equity + Sign-on + Refresh + Bonus + Benefits + PTO |
| Walk-Away Number | Know your floor. If below, decline gracefully. |
| "We don't have budget" | "What about equity/sign-on/refresh/benefits/timeline?" |
| Timeline Pressure | "I need [3-5 days] to review properly. This is a big decision." |
| Current Salary Question | Illegal in CA/CO/NY/WA/etc. "I'm targeting $X based on market." |
